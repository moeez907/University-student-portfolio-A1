Get Involved Today

Joining a university club is one of the best decisions you can make as a student. Whether you are passionate about technology, sports, culture, or public speaking, there is a club waiting for you. Follow the simple steps below to get started.

Steps to Join

1

Browse Clubs

Visit our Club Categories page to explore the different types of clubs available. Find one that matches your interests and goals.

2

Attend an Intro Session

Each club hosts introductory sessions at the start of every semester. Attend one to learn about the club activities, culture, and expectations.

3

Fill the Registration Form

Complete the club registration form available at the Student Affairs Office or online through the university portal. Bring your valid student ID.

4

Start Participating

Once registered, attend regular meetings, contribute to projects, and take part in events. The more you engage, the more you grow.

Participation Guidelines

📋

Registration Required

All students must formally register with a club before participating in official events and competitions.

📅

Regular Attendance

Members are expected to attend at least 75% of scheduled meetings to remain in active standing.

🤝

Respect and Teamwork

Treat fellow members with respect. Collaboration and inclusive behavior are at the core of every club.

🎓

Academics First

Club activities should complement your studies, not replace them. Maintain a minimum GPA of 2.5 to stay eligible.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I join more than one club?

Yes! Students are welcome to join multiple clubs as long as they can manage their time effectively and meet attendance requirements.

Is there a fee to join?

Most clubs are free to join. Some specialized clubs may have a small semester fee to cover materials or event costs.

When can I join?

Club registrations open at the beginning of each semester. However, some clubs accept members year-round. Check with the club coordinator.

What if I want to start a new club?

Submit a proposal to the Student Affairs Office with at least 10 interested members and a faculty advisor. Approval typically takes about 2 weeks.
